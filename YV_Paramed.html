WHAT IS YV?

YV is an emergency paramedic app that assists users to connect instantly to paramedic services conveniently on their mobile phones. The service links patients to emergency medical transport timely and within a safe environment.
We have a vision to lead, empower and impact pre-hospital care through technological innovative products that drive profits, enhance outcomes and ensure quality care to citizens.
We aim to eventually develop a conductive, competitive & enhanced ecosystem within the paramedic industry, that works together towards rendering efficient care.

The Current Problem

The Birth Of the product


South Africa currently faces a huge challenge with the shortage of both skills and shortages in ambulances which have resulted in longer waiting times for patients or no arrival at all irrespective of environment setting, urban or rural.


The Problem


Call Centres
Inadequately staffed & trained. Inherent problems: security, location accuracy/coordination, expensive to maintain. And is limited to voice (Prejudice against +/- 1 725 357 mute & deaf community)

No Universal EMS Number
Multiple emergency numbers: 112 / 10117 / 082 / ER24 / NetCare / Maponya911 etc. No aggregated services to coordinate private & government.

Fragmented & Limited Pre-Hospital Transport
Limited & inflexible pre-hospital transportation.


YV's Solution

The Solution


Automated Call Centre & Dispatch
A customized automated call centre & dispatch service. NB: Accommodating the +/- 1 725 357 mute & deaf vulnerable group.

Integrated Service / Resources Coordination
Your one dial for efficient medical transport.

Flexible Transport Convenient platform for booking & scheduling medical transport, for emergency & non-emergency.

Our Aims

Medical Transport Accessibility


Improve medical transport accessability for those in urban and rural setting, including enhancing services for the disabled group & pregnant women.


Universal EMS Platform


Coordinating EMS services per jurisdiction & province to enhance service and improve response times.


Enhance research output on EMS


South Africa and the African diaspora lack robust data in EMS.


Timing

Favourable Legislation and Policies/NHI


South Africa is currently in the 14 year plan to implement NHI, the policy favours a centralised call center between private and public EMS to foster a connected environment of resource sharing.


Increment in non-voice customer service engagement


This is a decline in voice based customer services and 87% increase in non-voice customer service engagement. Companies using voice & non-voice medium have seen a significant increase in revenue increment.


Our Target Market

Customer: Paramedic Companies B2B and EMS Departments B2G.

There is +/- 690 paramedic companies in SA, over +/- 1000 medical vehicles in all 9 provinces of SA.


Consumers: Disabled | The Elderly | Chemotherapy | Pregnancy

The overburden of disease and covid19 has exacerbated the need for reliable medical transport.
